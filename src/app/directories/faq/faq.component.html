Frequently Asked Questions

Q What is Ask Apollo?

A

Ask Apollo is an appointment scheduler to facilitate seeking confirming and fulfilling appointments for doctor consultations health checks and diagnostic services at any of the Apollo Hospitals across the group through the website or by calling the dedicated Ask Apollo. National telephone number -1-860-500-1066 .

Q How do I book an appointment for myself/family members/friends with a doctor at Apollo Hospitals?

A

Please log on to https://www.askapollo.com and follow simple instructions to book an appointment with your doctor. You may also call the Ask Apollo National telephone number -1-860-500-1066 to book an appointment. For any Apollo hospital in Andhra Pradesh Karnataka Tamil Nadu New Delhi Pune Kolkata and A hmedabad our customer care support team will take care of your appointment. You may book your appointment online minimum 2 days prior or call the Ask Apollo National telephone number 1 hr prior to required slot. Appointments can be booked subject to availability of slot.

Q Can I cancel or reschedule my appointment?

A

Once you book an appointment you will be provided with an Ask Apollo ID identification no and password. You may log in with the ID and password and manage your appointments. You can cancel or reschedule your appointment with the same or a different doctor depending on the availability of doctor slot on the chosen date.

Q How can I make sure that my appointment is confirmed?

A

Once you book an appointment you will be mailed a confirmation of the same which will have all the necessary details of your appointment (appointment date time specialty and doctors name). You will also receive a sms provided you have given your mobile number. You will receive a reminder sms a day before your appointment. If for any reason your doctor is not available on that particular day our customer care team will intimate you by phone or E-mail prior to the appointment and reschedule your appointment. In case of emergency please approach our Emergency department.

Q Is an E-mail -ID compulsory for booking on Ask Apollo?

A

E-mail ID is mandatory for booking through the website. If you do not have an E-mail ID you can give a family members ID. The purpose of providing an E-mail ID is for easy communication that may be required from time to time. It is always advisable to provide an E-mail ID. However for telephonic booking E-mail ID is not mandatory.

Q Do I need to fill the details again when I book an appointment the next time?

A

You do not need to provide your details again when you book an appointment the next time. With the login ID and password you can book another appointment for yourself or any other family member. But you have to fill the patient details if he/she is new to Ask Apollo.

Q Which are the cities where this facility is currently available?

A

Currently this facility is available at Ahmedabad, Aragonda, Bangalore, Bhiali, Bhubaneshwar, Bilaspur, Calicut, Chengalpattu, Chennai, Delhi, Ghaziabad, Hyderabad,Kakinada, Karaikudi, Karimnagar, Karur, Kolkata, Ludhiana, Madurai, Mysore, NewDelhi, Noida, Pune, Ranchi and Vizag.This facility will be introduced in all other cities wherever there is an Apollo Hospital.

Q Can I pay online?

A

Currently the online payment facility is not available. You have to pay your bill amount at the respective hospital. The online payment facility will be available soon.

Q Do you have a separate delivery channel at the hospital for Ask Apollo?

A

There is no separate delivery channel for Ask Apollo. It is integrated with the general operations of the hospital.

Q What do I do when I reach the hospital?

A

Please approach the reception/ Ask Apollo help desk regarding your appointment. The respective staff will guide you to the consultant. We request you to reach the hospital 15 minutes prior to your appointment for completing the registration process. You have to pay registration fee in addition to the consultation fee. The registration number is valid for a period of 10 years. Should you visit again do not pay anything for registration; simply produce your UHID card.

A qualified dermatologist concentrates on multiple activities like acting as consultants to other specialists, seeing patients in private or in hospitals, teaching and carrying out research work on their medical field. 6 Face Washing Mistakes that are Damaging Your Skin, Everyday! Face-washing may be a simple everyday task that requires no great effort on your part. But when it?s not done right, it causes significant harm to your skin, each day. The mistakes that you may make while washing your face could lead to issues like irritation, dryness and even wrinkles. Read on to find out how you can avoid making these common errors, and save your skin from blemishes, ageing and lustreless look. 1. Using the wrong cleanser The use of the wrong cleanser can throw your ph levels out of balance. The optimal ph balance of your skin is 5.5ph. This is the ideal level at which the thin protective layer on the surface of the skin (called acid mantle) can function to keep germs, toxins and bacteria at bay. You should therefore be picking your cleanser based on your skin type as it would come with the right ph balance. 2. Not cleaning your hands before touching the face Always clean the germs and dirt of your hands first before moving onto your face. Ignoring this crucial first step can cause you to transfer all the bacteria and dirt of your hands on your face, giving rise to common skin problems such as acne, pimples and blackheads. 3. Using a pre-used washcloth To avoid the filth from clogging your pores, always clean the washcloth that you use with an antiseptic to keep germs and bacteria at bay. Once you're done washing your face, don't rub the cloth all over your face but pat dry it. 4. Washing face using with hot water When it comes to the water that you splash on your face, you should always use lukewarm or room temperature water. This is because hot water leaves your skin dry as it washes away the skin's essential oils; and cold water can break the capillaries (fine blood vessels) of your skin. In contrast, mildly lukewarm water will keep your skin hydrated and healthy. 5. Not washing the face twice a day Just as you shouldn't ignore this important skin care habit that keeps your skin clean, you also shouldn't go overboard with it. Excessive face-washing can hamper the natural oil production of your skin, giving rise to dryness and irritation as a result. Your skin requires the natural oil it produces to keep it smooth and supple. Ideally, you should wash your face twice daily - once in the morning for removing the bacteria and dirt that clogged your pores, and again at night before going to bed. 6. Rushing through the routine Lastly, don't rush through your cleansing routine. Make sure to properly rinse your face of cleanser completely, otherwise the residues would cause your skin to dry up later on, leading to skin irritation. It is therefore very important to pay attention to all the little details that make up your face-washing routine. By doing it right, you get to achieve the clear and blemish-free skin that you always wanted.
